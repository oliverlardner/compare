What this calculator is actually doing

This panel (break-even snapshot): uses the token volumes you type in above to estimate your current cloud spend, then compares that to your local electricity cost.
Monthly cloud spend = ((input tokens × input price) + (output tokens × output price)) × days per month.
Monthly local spend = electricity + any extra monthly cost you add.
Break-even months = hardware upfront cost ÷ (monthly cloud spend − monthly local spend).

The sweet-spot heatmap (Apple AU pricing tab): answers the related question — how many tokens can this hardware generate locally on the chosen model, and what would that same volume cost on the cloud?
Output tokens/month = tok/s × 3600 × active hours/day × utilisation % × days/month. Input tokens = output tokens × input:output ratio. Both priced at the cloud API rates from this panel.
The tok/s figure is decode (generation) speed. Utilisation accounts for idle time, thinking time, and the fact that real usage is bursty — not a continuous token furnace. A realistic utilisation is 10–30%.
Cloud value = net monthly savings + electricity. Break-even = hardware price ÷ net monthly savings. This is a ceiling — tune active hours and utilisation % to match your real workflow.

Neither calculation assumes a local model matches the quality of GPT-5.4 or Claude Opus. They only ask: if your workload could move local, when does the hardware pay for itself?
